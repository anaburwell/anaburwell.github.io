This project is located in New Orleans, Louisiana and was a post-Katrina relief effort.
Individuals outside the community felt like not enough was being done and their donated
funds allowed this project to happen. Most housing that was going up at this time was
focused
on
timeliness
and
efficiency--
not
design.
With
more
of
a
focus
on
design,
Alligator House
was still a low-cost project and accommodated for New Orleans Culture.
The project included a shotgun style, a front stoop, and hurricane resistance. The
shotgun style, a layout of one room after another
, is very common in New Orleans. A
front stoop is also fairly important to the culture as a place to sit and talk to natures.
Finally
, in response to site, the
Alligator House
was built to meet hurricane resistance
requirements
with
insulated
windows
for
thermal
efficiency
and
metal
siding
for
durability
.

Design Concept Statement
This
design
is
based
on
the
Enthusiast,
a
term
defined
as
Y
our
interests
take
you
far
and
wide.
Y
ou
immerse
yourself in everything you do in order to experience life to the fullest.
Y
oure your own person,
open-minded,
and
forever
busy
trying
new
things.
Another
inspiration
is
Composition
VII,
a
painting
by
W
assily
Kandinsky
.
These
inspirations
influence
this
design
in
its
composition,
morphology
,
materiality
,
light,
movement,
and
elevation.
These
inspirations
evoke
lighting
that
is
bright
and
specialized,
and
call
for
a
lot
of
mixed
materiality
utilizing
bright
colors.
This
design
also
plays
on
the
concept
of
forever
busy
trying
new
things
by
encouraging
movement
through
multiple
specialized
areas.
Compositionally
,
this
means
that
while
there
are
many
rooms,
they
open
up
to
each
other and can be visually linked.
F
urthermore,
there is also visual
movement in the design that can be seen in the
stacked
glass
walls
that
open
up
to
the
library
and
closet.
So,
while
remaining
stationary
,
there
is
a
sense
of
the
dynamic
and
enthusiastic
movement,
heavily
found
in
Composition
VII.
Another
aspect
of
the
design
s
unique
composition
is
the
ceiling beams that emphasize the middle of the space by connecting at a point.
The morphology of the space ranges
far
and
wide:
having
both
curvilinear
and
rectilinear
aspects.
The
raised
floor
leads
the
user
into
a
distinctly
separate
space,
which
is
intimately
connected
to
the
endless
possibilities
beyond
the
large
windows.
There
is
a
lot
of
open
window
space
so
the
enthusiast
can
really
experience
life,
immersing
themselves
in
the
busy
outside
world.
Finally
,
throughout the design,
there is an emphasis on verticality so the enthusiast is always looking up toward new things and
sees the space to grow
.
